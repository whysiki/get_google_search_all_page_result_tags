    HVAC FAQs

    Are Air-rite Solutions HVAC Contractors?

    Air-rite Solutions are fully licensed and insured to work on projects as HVAC Contractors for builders, project managers and property managers. If you’d like to enquire about sub-contracting Air-rite Solutions for your next project or maintenance contract, please reach out to us on our contact page.

    Does Air-rite do commercial HVAC design in-house?

    Yes, all commercial HVAC designs undertaken by Air-rite Solutions are conducted in-house by our licensed team. This way, we can maintain a streamlined process between our commercial HVAC designers, mechanics and technicians.

    To see more of our existing commercial HVAC designs, be sure to check out our Brisbane HVAC projects.

    Why is it important to choose the right commercial HVAC partner?

    If you are in need of high-quality commercial HVAC services for your Brisbane or Southeast Queensland business, then it’s essential you choose a partner that understands the unique needs of this area. You will want a HVAC company that offers the full range of commercial air conditioning services. Many companies only focus on installation and maintenance, whereas Air-rite Solutions is certified to also design and supply high-quality commercial HVAC systems.

    What is included in a commercial air conditioning maintenance checklist?

    Air-rite’s HVAC technicians use a comprehensive commercial air conditioning maintenance checklist when undertaking maintenance work on our clients’ systems. These checklists include a list of parts and components that need to be checked on either a monthly, quarterly, or annual basis. This way, the health of the system will be maintained long after installation, saving our clients expensive costs on emergency repairs and the steep power bills that come as a result of an unmaintained system.

    What’s the importance of commercial air conditioning system design?

    Air-rite Solutions holds a QBCC air-conditioning and refrigeration Unlimited Design license which certifies us to create HVAC system designs for our clients. A thorough system design will take into account the type of space that the system has to service; the type of work that will be conducted in that space; what kind of HVAC system will best fit within the architectural design of the building; and a whole lot more.

    Do I need HVAC in Brisbane?

    Businesses in Brisbane have to cope with a harsh climate that can fluctuate between sweltering hot summer days and frosty winter mornings. An effective HVAC system will ensure that your team stays productive, your customers and clients will feel comfortable while in your premises, and you are meeting your legal requirements.

    For more information, read our article ‘Commercial Air Conditioning Brisbane: 4 Benefits’.

    What is ventilation and why do we need it?

    Ventilation is the transfer of clean air from outside to the inside of a building. Ventilation provides safe breathing air, while also eradicating harmful contaminants that can build up over time in unventilated locations. For the safety of your building’s occupants, effective ventilation is a requirement. HVAC systems are a great way to provide this ventilation and can be especially useful for rooms that don’t have access to a window attached to the outside of the building.

    To discover more about this topic, be sure to read our article ‘HVAC Systems: What is Ventilation and Why Do We Need It?’.

    How can I optimise my HVAC system?

    Optimising your HVAC system is a great way to increase its overall energy efficiency – saving you money when the next power bill comes through. There are several little adjustments you can make to optimise your HVAC system, such as changing the control algorithms to suit the workflow of your business or performing a night purge of any cool night air using natural ventilation. A bigger step to take would be to replace all of, or parts of, your system to a more modern and efficient solution.

    Why do I need a HVAC maintenance plan?

    If you are a building’s facility manager, then having a comprehensive HVAC maintenance plan will save you plenty of headaches in the long-term. Firstly, you have a legal obligation to organise regular maintenance checks on your building’s HVAC system to ensure the health of the occupants. Secondly, a HVAC maintenance plan will save you money in the long-term, as you won’t have to pay for regular repairs and the power bill will be cheaper.

    What obligations do I have to maintain my HVAC system?

    As a building’s facility manager, you have a legal obligation to maintain your HVAC system for the health and safety of that building’s occupants. This obligation falls under the Australian Standard AS/NZ 3666.2:2011 – Air-handling and water systems – Microbial Control, which you can read about in our article ‘Commercial Air Conditioning Service: Your Obligations’.

    What’s the difference between HVAC design, supply, installation, and maintenance?

    There are some key differences between HVAC design, supply, installation, and maintenance. Commercial HVAC design is the initial process of drawing up the plans for a new system. HVAC supply is when all of the necessary parts, components, and equipment are delivered to the building. From there, the system is installed into the building. And finally, HVAC maintenance occurs regularly to inspect the continual functionality of the system to avoid any potential breakdowns in the future.
