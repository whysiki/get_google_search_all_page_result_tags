Regular maintenance and timely tune-ups can be a great way to extend the life of your heating and air conditioning systems by helping to improve their efficiency and helping to catch any small issues before they have a chance to develop into something that could cause serious damage to your system. By investing in regular maintenance, you will likely save yourself a great deal of money in the long run.

The general consensus among professionals is that you should get your system maintained at least twice on a yearly basis; once before the summer gets into full swing, and once again in the fall before winter begins. This will ensure that your system is tuned up and ready to go before the months of the year when the most demand is going to be placed on it.

Good system efficiency begins with finding the right system for your home and making sure that everything is installed correctly. After that point, proper maintenance is going to be a major factor, as well as making sure that you change out your system's air filters on a regular basis. Clogged air filters can make it more difficult for your system to perform its job as it should, and all of that extra strain can really start to make a difference in how hard your system has to work in order to keep up with you and your family's heating and cooling demands.

There are a number of different factors that go into determining when the right time to replace your old system is, including things like overall system performance, as well as the level of efficiency at which your system is running. If you notice that your monthly heating or cooling bills just seem to continue rising, even though your use hasn't changed, or you notice that your system is struggling to keep your home at the level of comfort you and your family desire, it might be time to start shopping around for replacement options.
